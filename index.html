Demo for "Speaker-independent bone-conducted speech enhancement using SiGAN"

Authors: Liangliang Cheng, Jian Zhou, Liang Tao, and Hon Keung Kwan
 
Abstract: Compared to air-conducted (AC) speech, bone-conducted (BC) speech is free from environmental noise and thus can be used as an alternative for human-computer interaction. Unfortunately, the quality and intelligibility of bone-conducted speech are much lower than those of air-conducted speech due to the significant attenuation of high-frequency components from the low-pass filtering effect of the human tissue. This paper proposes a speaker-independent generative adversarial network for speaker-independent bone-conducted speech enhancement (SiGAN-BCSE). Specifically, a novel SiGAN is proposed for learning the nonlinear spectral mapping from BC speech to clean AC speech using an unpaired and unaligned parallel speech corpus as training data. By exploiting the cross-domain spectra similarity and semantic invariance between BC speech and AC speech, the proposed SiGAN can produce an accurate spectrum of AC speech for BC speech. Unlike existing methods that are specific-speaker oriented, the proposed SiGAN-BCSE is speaker-independent since it can be trained with training data comprising multiple speakers and can estimate clean AC speech for seen or unseen speakers. Compared with the speech quality and intelligibility of existing state-of-the-art methods, the SiGAN-BCSE gains 5.66%, 6.85%, and 11.70% of speech quality improvements and 7.69%, 8.53%, and 9.09% of speech intelligibility improvements on AEUCHSAC&BC-2017, TMHINT, and TMBCS datasets, respectively. In particular, the proposed SiGAN-BCSE obtains a similar performance for the unseen speaker as that of the seen speakers on the TMHINT dataset, indicating that the SiGAN-BCSE is speaker-independent.
